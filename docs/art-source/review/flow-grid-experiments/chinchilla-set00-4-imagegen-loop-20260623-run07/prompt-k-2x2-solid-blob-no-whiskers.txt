Create one review-only 4-frame sprite sheet for a Windows desktop pet app.

Primary goal:
- Extraction cleanliness and stable closed shapes are more important than animation detail.
- Make a clean parser-friendly 4-frame review sheet with no pinholes, no stray marks, no detached fragments, and no background contamination.
- This is for visual review and fixed-cell diagnostics only, not final production assets.

Canvas and layout:
- Square canvas, target 1024x1024.
- Use an invisible 2 rows x 2 columns layout.
- Intended cell size: 512x512 per cell.
- Place exactly one complete right-facing standard gray chinchilla in each cell.
- Keep every chinchilla fully inside its own cell, centered, with consistent camera distance, scale, and contact baseline.
- Each chinchilla should occupy about 40-45% of its cell width.
- Leave clean green margin around every animal on all sides.
- No visible grid lines, no labels, no numbers, no text.

Background:
- The entire background must be a flat, solid, pure chroma green color: #00ff00.
- Use #00ff00 only for the background.
- Do not use green anywhere inside the chinchilla.
- No gradients, no texture, no noise, no background shading, no vignette.
- No floor, no ground line, no shadow, no shelf, no scenery.

Style:
- Clean compact 2D game sprite style.
- Flat icon-like mascot proportions with solid block colors only.
- Minimalist closed-shape design.
- Low detail, readable closed silhouette.
- Not pixel art.
- Not realistic fur.
- Not a large detailed character illustration.
- No painterly shading, no fur texture, no fuzzy fur edges, no individual hair strands.
- Standard gray chinchilla: medium neutral gray coat, simple cream belly patch, rounded ears, compact body, attached curled thick tail.
- Clean black eye, small simple muzzle, closed mouth.
- No whiskers at all.
- Keep the same character identity, proportions, head size, ear size, body size, tail size, and color palette in all 4 frames.

Solid shape constraints:
- The chinchilla must read as one thick chunky mass.
- Body, head, ears, belly patch, feet, and tail must be solid closed shapes.
- No internal negative space.
- No thin strokes, no small gaps, no hairline cuts, no tiny holes.
- Limbs and tail must sit completely flush against the body.
- Tail must be one continuous attached thick curled shape, like a filled bean shape connected to the body.
- Feet must be tiny filled pads fused to the underside of the body, not separate objects.
- Belly patch must be a single solid cream area with no green openings.

Feet and underside:
- Each foot must be a small filled oval pad.
- Foot pads must be attached to and slightly overlapping the underside of the body.
- Keep foot pads simple, solid, closed, and chunky.
- Do not show lifted feet, separated feet, long thin legs, toes, toe gaps, or open green spaces between feet and belly.
- Keep the belly underside as one continuous closed shape.

Pose requirement:
- Create 4 controlled near-identical silhouette-safe pose phases, not a full walking cycle.
- Pose 1: neutral compact pose.
- Pose 2: tiny forward lean, same fused foot pads.
- Pose 3: tiny opposite lean, same fused foot pads.
- Pose 4: soft recovery pose leading back to pose 1.
- Pose differences should be subtle but visible in body lean and tail balance.
- Do not create a strong stride, passing pose, lifted feet, separated leg motion, or complex foot placement.
- Prioritize clean filled shapes over motion differences.

Hard constraints:
- Exactly 4 complete chinchillas total.
- Exactly one complete right-facing chinchilla per cell.
- No duplicate animals inside a cell.
- No extra animals, no cropped animals.
- No missing tail, no detached ears, no separated limbs.
- No sitting, no grooming, no jumping, no running, no scenery actions.
- No white fringe, no pale halo, no green halo, no semi-transparent-looking rim.
- No tiny green holes inside or between body parts.
- No transparent-looking gaps inside the animal.
- Final image must contain only the 4 chinchilla sprites on pure #00ff00 background.
