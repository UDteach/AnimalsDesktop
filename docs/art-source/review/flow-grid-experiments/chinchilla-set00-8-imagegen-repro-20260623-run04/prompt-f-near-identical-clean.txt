Create one review-only 8-frame sprite sheet for a Windows desktop pet app.

Primary goal:
- Extraction cleanliness is more important than animation quality or artistic detail.
- Make a clean, parser-friendly 8-frame review sheet with no matte defects, no pinholes, no stray marks, and no background contamination.
- This sheet is for visual review and fixed-cell diagnostics only, not final production assets.

Canvas and layout:
- Target canvas: 2048x1024.
- Use an invisible 2 rows x 4 columns layout.
- Intended cell size: 512x512 per cell.
- If the model does not follow the exact pixel size, still keep the same 2:1 overall aspect ratio and a clean equal 2x4 layout.
- Place exactly one complete right-facing standard gray chinchilla in each cell.
- Keep every chinchilla fully inside its own cell, centered, with consistent scale, camera distance, and contact baseline.
- Leave clear pure green space around every animal inside each cell.
- No visible grid lines, no labels, no numbers, no text.

Background:
- The entire background must be a flat, solid, pure chroma green color: #00ff00.
- Use #00ff00 only for the background.
- Do not use green anywhere inside the chinchilla.
- No gradients, no texture, no noise, no shading in the background, no vignette.
- No floor, no ground line, no shadow, no shelf, no scenery.

Matte and edge cleanliness:
- The animal must have a clean, closed, solid silhouette.
- The belly, feet, tail, ears, and body must be filled with continuous solid shapes.
- No pinholes anywhere inside the body, belly, feet, tail, ears, or around the legs.
- No tiny green holes inside or between body parts.
- No transparent-looking gaps inside the animal.
- No broken gaps around the belly line, foot contact area, tail base, or tail curve.
- No white fringe, no pale halo, no green halo, no semi-transparent-looking rim.
- No detached specks, no isolated dots, no dust-like marks, no loose fragments.
- Keep all feet, paws, ears, whiskers, and tail attached to the body.
- Keep whiskers short, simple, and attached to the muzzle.
- Avoid fine hair strands, fuzzy edges, texture noise, and realistic fur detail.
- Avoid background color contamination near the silhouette and near the invisible cell boundaries.

Animal design and style:
- Clean small 2D game sprite style.
- Not pixel art.
- Not realistic fur.
- Simple flat cel-shaded shapes with minimal texture.
- Cute compact desktop mascot sprite, easy to read at small size.
- Standard gray chinchilla: medium neutral gray coat, cream belly, rounded ears, compact body, curled bushy tail, small separated feet.
- Clean black eye, small muzzle, closed mouth.
- Keep the exact same character identity, proportions, head size, ear size, body size, tail size, and color palette in all 8 frames.

Pose requirement:
- Create 8 near-identical clean review poses, not a strong walking animation.
- The poses may have only extremely tiny natural variation.
- Keep the belly, feet, and tail shapes simple, closed, attached, and solid.
- Do not create visible stride, separated leg motion, lifted feet, or complex foot placement.
- Do not create frame-by-frame gait extremes.
- Prioritize clean filled shapes over motion differences.
- Keep forepaws tucked and compact.
- Keep the tail as one continuous attached curled shape.

Hard constraints:
- Exactly 8 complete chinchillas total.
- Exactly one complete right-facing chinchilla per cell.
- No duplicate animals inside a cell.
- No extra animals, no cropped animals.
- No missing tail, no detached ears, no separated limbs.
- No sitting, no grooming, no jumping, no running, no scenery actions.
- Final image must contain only the 8 chinchilla sprites on pure #00ff00 background.
